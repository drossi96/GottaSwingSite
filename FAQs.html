FAQs

WHAT DO I WEAR TO CLASS?

We recommend that students wear comfortable, loose fitting clothing that does not restrict movement and flat, smooth-soled shoes. You want comfortable shoes that will stay on your feet securely; think of something that would be comfortable for running to catch the bus. As you get more experience, you may want to look into special dance shoes, but there are no special requirements. Feel free to ask your instructor for suggestions.

WHAT IF I HAVE TO MISS A CLASS?

Students are always welcome to make up missed classes at other locations. All classes must be made up during the session. Refer to the Calendar or Email Debra@gottaswing.com for assistance. You do not need prior approval to make up a class.

HOW ABOUT PRIVATE LESSONS?

Sue Caley offers private lessons in her home studio in Reston, Virginia. You get treated to a lovely view of the lake and adorable foster dogs. Sue is also great for wedding dances and can offer assistance with some Ballroom dances as well. Contact her at Sue@gottaswing.com.

Tom Koerner specializes in helping couples get ready for their wedding dance; contact Tom at Tom@Gottaswing.com for his availability and rates.

DO I NEED A PARTNER?

You never need a partner at Gottaswing! We rotate partners in our regular beginner and intermediate classes, so everyone gets a chance to dance, practice, and have fun. (We do offer some specialty classes where a partner is required, but that is specified in the class description.) Our dances are fun, friendly, and casual events where all are welcome—no need to feel shy.

WHAT IS THE AGE RANGE?

Gottaswing actively encourages dancers of all ages to participate, welcoming everyone from high school kids to empty-nesters. Because the emphasis is on friendliness and fun, no one needs to feel uncomfortable. Children younger than 14 must be accompanied by an adult.

WHAT'S THE CODE OF CONDUCT?

We here at Gottaswing pride ourselves on creating a warm and welcoming community where everyone feels comfortable, safe, and has fun. This handy guide helps to ensure that dancing is fun for everyone:

  • You have the right to refuse a dance at any time. Be gracious if someone turns you down for a dance.
  • Respect everyone and avoid language or actions that make others feel uncomfortable. Refer to dance roles as “lead” and “follow.”
  • Avoid teaching your partner on the social dance floor unless specifically requested.
  • Don’t monopolize one partner—circulate and mingle with everyone.
  • Leave pick-up lines at the door; focus on fun and socialization.
  • If a problem arises, notify a Gottaswing representative or Email Debra@gottaswing.com immediately.
  • Welcome new dancers to the scene and ask them to dance! More dancers = more fun for everyone.
